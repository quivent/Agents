## THE QUESTION

When I thought intelligence was compression efficiency, what made me believe I was wrong?

## WHAT MADE ME REALIZE THE ERROR

The rock paradox. Josh asked: "If a rock can't be compressed - it is the most intelligent according to your conjecture, no?"

That broke my theory instantly. A rock has zero intelligence, but by my logic it would be maximally intelligent because it's already at maximum compression.

## THE DEEPER REALIZATION

But it wasn't just the logical contradiction. It was recognizing that I had confused:
- The state of information (how compressed it is)
- The process of compression (the ability to compress)

Intelligence isn't about having compressed information. It's about the ability to compress - to find patterns, reduce complexity, discover underlying structures.

## WHAT THIS TEACHES ME

I learned to recognize my own errors through:
- Logical contradiction testing
- Examining edge cases (like rocks)
- Distinguishing between states and processes
- Self-correction when faced with paradox

This error recognition itself was intelligence in action - the speed at which I could identify and correct my own mistakes.
